About Puerto Princesa

History

Puerto Princesa has a rich history shaped by its indigenous communities...

Puerto Princesa's history dates back to pre-colonial times when it was inhabited by indigenous tribes like the Tagbanwa and Batak. It became a Spanish settlement in 1872 and later developed into a thriving city known for its natural wonders. The city played a significant role during World War II and continues to preserve its cultural heritage while embracing modern development.

Culture

The people of Puerto Princesa are warm and welcoming...

Puerto Princesa's culture is deeply rooted in its indigenous traditions. Festivals like the Pista Y Ang Kagueban celebrate the unity of its diverse communities, while local crafts and cuisine showcase its unique heritage. The city is also home to vibrant art scenes and traditional music that reflect the creativity and resilience of its people.

Stewardship

Puerto Princesa prioritizes environmental conservation...

As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Puerto Princesa is committed to preserving its natural resources. Initiatives like the Underground River protection program ensure sustainable tourism practices. The city actively promotes eco-friendly policies and community involvement in environmental preservation, making it a model for sustainable development worldwide.
